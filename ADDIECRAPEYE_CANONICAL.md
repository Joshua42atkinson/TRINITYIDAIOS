## The 12 Stations

### ADDIE — *Extract the Wisdom* (Cars 1–5)

| # | Letter | Station | Definition |
|---|--------|---------|------------|
| 1 | **A** | Analyze | Understand the problem and requirements |
| 2 | **D** | Design | Plan the solution architecture |
| 3 | **D** | Develop | Build the solution |
| 4 | **I** | Implement | Deploy and integrate |
| 5 | **E** | Evaluate | Assess outcomes and learning |

> **Attribution:** ADDIE was developed by the **Center for Educational Technology at Florida State University** (c. 1975) for the U.S. Army. The acronym was first published by Michael Schlegel in 1995. It is the most widely used instructional systems design (ISD) model in the world.

### CRAP — *Place the Wisdom* (Cars 6–9)

| # | Letter | Station | Definition |
|---|--------|---------|------------|
| 6 | **C** | Contrast | Make elements stand out through visual difference |
| 7 | **R** | Repetition | Use consistent design elements for cohesion |
| 8 | **A** | Alignment | Place elements to create structure and order |
| 9 | **P** | Proximity | Group related elements together |

> **Attribution:** CRAP was coined by **Robin Williams** (graphic designer, not the actor) in *The Non-Designer's Design Book* (Peachpit Press, 1994). ISBN 978-0133966152. These are foundational visual design principles taught in most design programs worldwide.

### EYE — *Refine the Wisdom* (Cars 10–12)

| # | Letter | Station | Definition |
|---|--------|---------|------------|
| 10 | **E** | Envision | See the end state; imagine the finished product |
| 11 | **Y** | Yoke | Connect disparate pieces into a unified whole |
| 12 | **E** | Evolve | Iterate, improve, and transcend the current version |

> **Attribution:** EYE is an **original contribution by Joshua Atkinson** (2026) as part of the Trinity ID AI OS project at Purdue University. It is not derived from any existing instructional design framework. EYE extends ADDIE+CRAP by adding a reflective vision-iteration loop that completes the 12-station cycle.

## PEARL — The Focusing Agent

**P**erspective **E**ngineering **A**esthetic **R**esearch **L**ayout

The PEARL is the per-project alignment document. It captures:
- **WHO** the learner/user is
- **WHAT** they need to learn/build
- **WHY** it matters
- **HOW** it should be delivered (medium)

> Source: `pearl.rs` in trinity-protocol

## PARTY — The AI Agent Crew

The PARTY is the RPG-style team of AI agents that execute the work:

| Role | Emoji | Function |
|------|-------|----------|
| **Conductor** | 🎭 | Guides conversation and delegates tasks |
| **Engineer** | ⚙️ | Generates and modifies code |
| **Evaluator** | 📊 | Reviews quality against rubrics |
| **Artist** | 🎨 | Creates designs and narratives |
| **Brakeman** | 🛡️ | Tests and validates safety |
| **Visionary** | 👁️ | Analyzes visuals and UI |

> PARTY is not a separate acronym — it refers to the RPG party metaphor where multiple AI agents collaborate as a team, each with a specialized role.

## How the Pieces Fit

```
ADDIECRAPEYE (WHAT to do — 12 stations)
     ↕
   PEARL     (WHAT to focus on — per-project)
     ↕
   PARTY     (WHO does the work — AI agents)
```

The user progresses through the 12 ADDIECRAPEYE stations. At each station, the PEARL keeps them aligned with their project goals. The PARTY agents provide scaffolded assistance based on the user's skill level (Character Sheet).
